3 · Lyrics & singing faces

Paste or type the lyrics (one line per sung line), or load them from a text file. SimpleShow spreads them across the vocal parts of the song, breaks every word down into mouth-shape phonemes automatically, and casts your faces like a real act — a lead soloist or duet up front, everyone joining in on the chorus.

Lyrics

.txt / .lrc — a timestamped .lrc brings its line timing along
searches the free lrclib.net database (sends only artist & title)

Check the sync on step 5's live preview — the current word is highlighted as it plays. For the best line timing, use "Find synced lyrics online" or a timestamped .lrc: every line then starts exactly where a human synced it, and only the words inside each line are placed automatically. If everything is consistently early or late (a different edit of the song), drag the timing offset.

Face casting

Your lead (or duet) sings the verses; sections detected as chorus bring in every face as backup. Every word is broken down into Preston Blair mouth shapes (AI, E, O, U, FV, L, MBP, WQ, etc) right here — the exported Lyrics timing track carries lines, words, and phonemes, so faces sing in xLights with no manual breakdown step. Hover a line below to see its phonemes.

4 · Generate the show

Pick a style and intensity. SimpleShow choreographs the display the way a sequencer does: model groups carry sweeps, chases and call-and-response; only a few prop families are loud at once so the hits land; special props stay dark until the ramps. Don't like this show? Roll a new seed.

Show style

Layers & moves

Layered mode is how the pro sequences read: each active prop family keeps a dim wash of color on its bottom layer while chases, pulses and sweeps play on top (blended with Max, both here and in the exported .xsq). Untick a move to take it out of the vocabulary for this show — then reroll the seed.

Fix the lyrics

Automatic timing won't be perfect. Click a line below to select it (and jump the player there), drag it to move it, nudge it by 50 ms, contract or expand its timing, snap its start to the playhead, change which face sings it and in what color, or split it apart at any word. Everything you fix is saved in this browser for this song — regenerate the show as often as you like and your timing stays.

Export

Import into xLights: put the .xsq in your show folder next to xlights_rgbeffects.xml, open it in xLights, and point it at your audio file when prompted. The Lyrics timing track already carries the full phoneme breakdown, so the singing faces have mouth shapes immediately — no manual breakdown needed.
